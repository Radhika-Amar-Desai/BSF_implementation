# Steering Diffusion Model

This section of the paper describes how BSFs were used to steer the image generation process of SDXL by modifying its internal representations during diffusion.

## VAE foundations

It is used to encode images.

Overall pipeline:

```
Image
    ↓
Encoder
    ↓
Mean (μ), Standard Deviation (σ)
    ↓
Sample latent vector z ~ N(μ, σ²)
    ↓
Decoder
    ↓
Image
```

We prefer Variational Autoencoders (VAEs) over standard Autoencoders because they encourage the latent space to follow a continuous Gaussian distribution. This prevents "holes" or disconnected regions in latent space and results in a smooth manifold from which realistic images can be generated by sampling.

---

## SDXL Core

The diffusion model operates **entirely in the VAE latent space**.

Instead of generating pixels directly, SDXL starts from a random Gaussian latent and gradually removes noise while conditioning on the text prompt.

Overall pipeline:

```
Random Gaussian latent zT
        ↓
UNet predicts noise ε
        ↓
Scheduler removes predicted noise
        ↓
Updated latent zT-1
        ↓
Repeat for many denoising steps
        ↓
Final latent z0
        ↓
VAE Decoder
        ↓
Generated Image
```

The U-Net is responsible for predicting the noise present in the current latent, while the scheduler uses this prediction to obtain a slightly cleaner latent. Repeating this process many times gradually moves the latent towards the manifold of realistic images that satisfy the text prompt.

---

## BSF and Steering

The authors do **not** modify the VAE latent directly, nor do they modify the weights of the U-Net.

Instead, BSF is trained on **an intermediate activation produced by a chosen layer of the frozen SDXL U-Net** (the paper uses layer `down.2.1`).

During image generation:

```
Current latent zt
        ↓
Frozen U-Net
        ↓
Intermediate activation h
        ↓
BSF Encoder
        ↓
Block representation

[z₁ | z₂ | z₃ | ... | zk]

        ↓
Modify only one concept block

[z₁ | z₂' | z₃ | ... | zk]

        ↓
BSF Decoder
        ↓
Modified activation h'
        ↓
Continue through remaining U-Net layers
        ↓
Predicted noise
        ↓
Scheduler
        ↓
Updated latent zt-1
```

Only **one block** corresponding to the desired concept is modified, while all remaining blocks are left unchanged. The modified block is then combined with the untouched blocks using the BSF decoder to reconstruct the complete activation tensor. This reconstructed activation is fed back into the U-Net, which continues its forward pass normally.

The steering is performed **during several denoising steps**, allowing the subsequent diffusion process to naturally refine the modification. Since only a single semantic block is altered, other factors such as pose, lighting, composition, and background are largely preserved.

This is fundamentally different from editing the final VAE latent. Instead of changing the image after diffusion has finished, BSF intervenes **during** the denoising process by modifying an internal representation used by the U-Net to predict noise. The altered prediction changes the subsequent diffusion trajectory, resulting in smooth and realistic semantic edits.
